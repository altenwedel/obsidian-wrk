# Asset briefing, VRK animation on the subsidy process

## Project context

The Veiligheidsregio Kennemerland (VRK) and its public health agency (GGD) commissioned a 60-second 2D animation about the subsidy process. The audience is roughly 1,600 employees of GGD and VRK Kennemerland: doctors, advisers, project leads, communications staff and quality officers. The working title is "Subsidieregelen doe je zo!" (How to handle subsidies the right way).

The film aims to make employees feel ownership of the subsidy process. It does so by showing two parallel worlds, GGD and Finance, working together as professional partners, rather than by warning people about administrative failure. The tone is positive, recognisable and warm.

The script and storyboard exist in Dutch. This briefing translates the visual side into English so you can work in your preferred language. All on-screen text and voice-over remains in Dutch.

## Visual direction

### Style

A continuation of the Studio Claro visual language: 2D flat illustration with warm, slightly hand-drawn lines, restrained palette, generous white space and a soft, inviting feel. Backgrounds in muted grey, foreground figures and key objects in full colour. This focus technique helps viewers process what matters without being distracted.

Avoid:
- characters with closed eyes or downcast looks (we want eye contact with the viewer)
- a stern or formal feel (the male figures in our 2022 OR animation read as too austere)
- purple as a corporate base colour (a previous brief mentioned purple, this has been corrected to dark blue)

Aim for:
- open eyes and a friendly, approachable feel
- a sense of partnership between the two main characters
- visual lightness, positive mood, professional but human

### Colour palette

| Role | Name | HEX | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Corporate base | Dark blue | #1E1C5E | Frames, gizmo, key objects, headlines, dominant accent |
| GGD support | Orange | #F58A07 | Accents on the GGD adviser, GGD-context scenes, callouts |
| Light accent | Light blue | #E7F5FE | Soft fills, secondary surfaces |
| Soft accent | Light purple | #EFE7F2 | Sparingly, only where light fills are needed |
| Confirmation | Green | use a moderate, warm green | Approval ticks, confirmations, completion |
| Do not use as base | Purple | #5C0E81 | Only as small accent if absolutely needed |

Backgrounds neutral grey, around #F2F2F2 to #E5E5E5, slightly cool. Foreground objects in the colours above with clear contrast.

### Typography in image

When text appears in image (titles, on-screen labels), use Arial Bold or Barlow Bold in dark blue #1E1C5E. Keep on-screen text minimal, voice-over carries the message.

### Mood

Confidence and care. Viewers should feel that the people on screen are competent and warm, not bureaucratic. The film celebrates small acts of professionalism that keep public health services running.

## Recurring characters

### Character 1, GGD adviser (main)

Female, around 30 to 40 years old. Reference photos provided by the client (see `260427-fotos-ggd-adviseur.md` in source documents): warm, smiling, approachable.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Posture | Relaxed and confident, never rigid |
| Expression | Open eyes, gentle smile, makes eye contact with the viewer in key moments |
| Hair | Mid-length, natural colour (the references show blonde and curly brown; pick what fits the rest of the cast best) |
| Wardrobe | Smart-casual, not formal: a soft top in a neutral or light tone, simple trousers. Avoid suits or strict office wear |
| Accessory | Briefcase with campaign materials, sometimes a clipboard or laptop |
| Colour accent | Orange #F58A07 highlights (GGD support colour), used subtly on clothing, badge or accessory |

### Character 2, Finance subsidy adviser (second)

Equally warm and collegial, slightly different visual cue so the viewer can distinguish the two. Female or male is open, but pick one and keep it consistent. Aim for an inclusive cast across the film.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Posture | Engaged, often pointing at documents, leaning in to share information |
| Expression | Open and helpful, nods often, makes eye contact with the GGD adviser and with the viewer |
| Wardrobe | Smart-casual, slightly more office-coded than the GGD adviser (for example, a soft jacket or a buttoned shirt) |
| Accessory | A folder or tablet with budget data, sometimes a calculator icon |
| Colour accent | Dark blue #1E1C5E highlights (VRK corporate colour), creating visual contrast with the GGD adviser |

The two characters appear together from scene 5 onwards. They are equals: same height, same eye level, similar warmth.

## Recurring objects

### Object 1, briefcase with campaign materials

A friendly, sturdy briefcase. When opened, it shows a short stack of flyers, a rolled-up poster, a small lesson kit and a few loose cards. All shapes are generic, no readable text. The briefcase is the visual anchor for "the campaign is ready". From scene 4 onwards the same briefcase also visualises funding, with stacks of euro notes appearing inside it and the case dividing into three labelled compartments. There is no separate money briefcase.

### Object 2, gizmo (process indicator)

The narrative spine of the film. A small, friendly device with five segments arranged in a circle or in a row. Each segment lights up as the story moves through the corresponding phase: 1 Apply, 2 Execute, 3 Wrap up, 4 Account for, 5 Close.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| Form | Stylised dial or chevron-shaped row, depending on what reads best in 2D |
| Material | Clean, slightly tactile, with light shadowing |
| Colour | Dark blue base #1E1C5E, segments light up in orange #F58A07 (GGD signal) and green for "complete" moments |
| Position in frame | Top right corner from scene 5 onwards, scaled up for emphasis when a segment activates |

## Scene-by-scene briefing

### Scene 1, title card

Single frame. Working title in Dutch: "Subsidieregelen doe je zo!". Set in Arial Bold or Barlow Bold, dark blue, centred on a light background. Small Studio Claro and VRK logos in the lower margin if appropriate. Mood: opening, anticipatory, friendly.

### Scene 2, introducing the main character

The GGD adviser stands behind a simple table. She makes eye contact with the viewer, gentle smile. Her hands rest on a closed briefcase, ready to open it. Background is a soft grey workplace setting (could be a meeting room or community centre, kept abstract). Foreground in full colour.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Slight eye-level, medium shot |
| Lighting | Soft, warm |
| Action | She slides the briefcase forward, slight smile |
| Mood | Pride, calm confidence |

### Scene 3, opening the briefcase

Top-down view of the briefcase. Lid opens slowly. Inside: stack of flyers, rolled poster, lesson kit, loose cards. All generic shapes. Bottom right of the case shows the gizmo, ready to be picked up. Only one briefcase in this scene, no second case.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Top-down |
| Animation | Lid opens with soft click sound |
| Mood | Reveal, satisfaction |

### Scene 4, three forms of funding

Camera stays on the open briefcase. The campaign materials morph into stacks of euro notes inside the briefcase. Then the inside of the briefcase divides into three labelled compartments by thin lines: "Rijk" (national government), "gemeente" (municipality) and "subsidies". The three compartments hold their own euro stacks. At the moment "without funding" all stacks vanish from all three compartments, leaving the briefcase empty. After that beat, the three compartments return clearly to camera, restating the structure of where funding comes from. At the end, a hand reaches in and picks up the gizmo.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Animation | Materials morph to euro stacks; compartment lines slide in; stacks disappear and the empty compartments return |
| Colour | Euro stacks in soft, illustrated greens and blues; compartment labels in dark blue; lines neutral grey |
| Mood | Brief tension at "without funding", then resolved when the structure returns and the hand picks up the gizmo |
| Note | No price tags or outline-only items in this scene; we work with money in compartments |

### Scene 5, introducing the gizmo and the Finance adviser

The GGD adviser holds the gizmo up and places it in the top right of the frame. Segment 1 lights up. She fades back slightly, and the Finance adviser appears next to her. Both look at the viewer, then at each other. This is the moment partnership is established.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Composition | Two characters side by side, equal height |
| Colour | Gizmo in dark blue with segment 1 in orange |
| Mood | Welcoming, "this is a duo, not a solo" |

### Scene 6, phase 1, applying

Two micro-scenes inside this phase.

Sub-scene 6a, preparation: GGD adviser and Finance adviser sit together at a desk. Three preparation elements are visible on the table, taken from Lidya's bijlage 5 (step 1):

- A document labelled "Projectbegroting" (project budget), with stylised columns of figures.
- A small sheet labelled "Uurtarief" (hourly rate), showing a circled amount.
- A simple sketch labelled "Belanghebbenden" (stakeholders), with a few stick figures and arrows.

Finance adviser points at a line on the budget; GGD adviser nods. The labels are visible but small, the voice-over does not name them. The mood is light and collaborative, suggesting that good preparation pays off.

Sub-scene 6b, submission: GGD adviser at a laptop. The screen is semi-transparent, so we see a stylised application form on screen and the adviser's face behind it. She clicks "Submit". A green checkmark appears. Both characters smile.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Medium shot for both sub-scenes |
| Animation | Smooth cut between sub-scenes; checkmark animates in with a soft confirmation tone |
| Mood | Cooperative, encouraging |
| Note | The three preparation elements are visual cues only, not on-screen titles. Voice-over uses accessible language, formal terms only appear in scene 11 |

### Scene 7, phase 2, executing with monitoring

The number and rhythm of action moments in this scene is at your discretion, Claudia. The kernelementen below should all be visible, but you decide how many beats to give each one.

Beat 1, fieldwork: GGD adviser at a school. She speaks with a teacher, then we cut to a classroom with a poster. Children look at it. Cut to a brief interview with teenagers. Cut to an empty classroom afterwards. Posters in the background reference GGD campaigns (smoke-free, healthy living) but are stylised, not reproductions of any specific campaign.

Beat 2, monitoring during execution: split screen or short montage. The adviser in action next to her laptop with a budget chart showing "actual vs planned" and a small clock icon flashing briefly to underline "during, not after".

Beat 3, factureren (invoicing): close-up of the act of invoicing in the administration. Small green checkmarks appear at each booked entry. The emphasis is on doing the financial admin during the work, not afterwards.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Mix of medium and close-up |
| Animation | Quick, energetic cuts in beat 1; calmer in beats 2 and 3 |
| Mood | Competent, in control, professional |
| Decision | Claudia chooses how many sub-shots fit within the 60-second budget |

### Scene 8, phase 3, wrapping up

GGD adviser closes the briefcase. She makes eye contact with the viewer and glances at the gizmo. Beside her, a small stack of collected documents and a checklist visible.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Medium shot, slight angle |
| Animation | Briefcase closes with a soft click |
| Mood | Reflective, transitional |

### Scene 9, phase 4, accounting for, two worlds

The frame splits into two halves with a clean vertical line. Left, the GGD adviser. Right, the Finance adviser. Arrows show information flowing:

- An arrow from the GGD adviser to a stylised institution icon (subsidy provider) on the upper left.
- An arrow from the Finance adviser to a stylised auditor icon (loupe or magnifier) on the upper right.

In the centre, two stamps appear sequentially: "Voorschot" (Advance) and "Definitief" (Final). The final stamp lights up green when the chain is complete.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Full split-screen |
| Animation | Arrows fade in one at a time; stamps drop with a soft sound |
| Mood | Clarity, mutual responsibility, fairness |

### Scene 10, phase 5, closing

Segment 5 lights up. The gizmo slides back into the briefcase. The lid closes. The cycle has closed; no second briefcase is shown.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Top-down or side angle |
| Animation | Gizmo motion is smooth and satisfying |
| Mood | Completion |
| Note | Voice-over closes with "het geld is verantwoord en geregeld" (accounted for and arranged), not "received" |

### Scene 11, formal terminology and accessibility

This scene introduces the formal vocabulary employees will encounter in beschikkingen, on subsidy provider websites and in formal mails. The visual style mirrors the yellow highlight bars from Lidya's bijlage 5 (see references): a yellow bar with dark blue text, with a small icon to the left of each term.

The six formal terms appear one after the other, each with its own yellow bar:

1. Aanvraag (application)
2. Subsidieverlening (granting decision)
3. Bevoorschotting (advance payment)
4. Aanvraag subsidievaststelling (request for final settlement)
5. Subsidievaststelling (final determination)
6. Betaling (final payment)

Then two URL bars appear: ggdkennemerland.nl and vrk.nl, with a small text label "Vragen? Bel je collega van Financien of de subsidieadviseur" (Questions? Call your Finance colleague or the subsidy adviser).

| Element   | Note                                                                                                                                                                               |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Camera    | Wide flat, the bars stack vertically or slide in horizontally                                                                                                                      |
| Animation | Each yellow bar clicks in with a small chime; URL bars slide up at the end                                                                                                         |
| Mood      | Reassuring, like a small glossary moment                                                                                                                                           |
| Note      | The voice-over frames this as "klein woordenboekje" (a small glossary) so viewers understand why these unfamiliar terms appear. The icons are small and supportive, not decorative |
| Reference | Match Lidya's bijlage 5 styling closely, including the colour and proportions of the yellow bars                                                                                   |

### Scene 12, closing, partnership and field group

A real group composition. GGD adviser and Finance adviser stand in the centre, shoulder to shoulder, with open eyes and eye contact with the viewer. On either side, the people from the field join them: the teacher from scene 7, a few children and teenagers, and optionally a colleague care worker. Visually this shows the chain: the two professionals work together, and that is what makes the work possible for the people in the field who benefit. Background grey, foreground in full colour.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Eye level, slight zoom out, group composition |
| Animation | Backgrounds simplify; the group settles into a calm, balanced frame |
| Mood | Trust, partnership, visible chain from professionals to the people they serve |
| Composition tip | Aim for an inclusive cast in the group: mixed ages, mixed visual cues, but all warm |

### Scene 13, looking ahead

Wide shot. GGD adviser pulls the briefcase towards her. Beside her, the Finance adviser, a teacher colleague, children and teenagers. The team is visible without being crowded. The film ends on a sense of continuity.

| Element | Note |
|---|---|
| Camera | Wider, group composition |
| Animation | Slow pull-back |
| Mood | Continuity, anticipation |
| Voice-over | Single short line in Dutch: "Tijd voor een nieuwe campagne" (no follow-up about new goals) |
